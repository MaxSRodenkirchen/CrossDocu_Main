# Projecting onto a Glass Prism

A small experiment was conducted to investigate what happens when an interactive visual is projected through a glass prism. The main **prism ball** had a diameter of **4.5 centimeters** which is a bit small for the projector. We also tested working wit a **triangular prism** which is interesting as well but does not cause a nicer effect. 

### Documentation


### Conclusion

1. Even the slightest movements can cause instability 
2. The prism needs to stay stiff. Movement should only be intentional 
3. The bigger the prism the more you can still see the original projected image
4. More prisms = more complex effects
5. Even if you don't see the original visual you will still recognize that the visual is interactive. (In our case reacting to our body movement [[Webcam Movement Visualization in TD]])
